# Project Inspirations

These are possible directions, not a task list. They are here to help
contributors find useful angles without narrowing their imagination.

## Data Quality And Standardization

Possible directions:

- Ways to help contributors understand whether a dataset follows the schema
- Better explanations of missingness, completeness, and consistency
- Tools that help normalize field names or categorical values
- Workflows that help partners prepare data before submission
- Methods for comparing datasets across contexts without forcing one global
  interpretation

## Research Enablement

Possible directions:

- Reusable analysis patterns using synthetic data
- Metadata explorers that make datasets easier to understand
- Research question libraries
- Literature-to-data mapping approaches
- Templates for turning validated data into responsible research outputs

## Accessibility And Inclusion

Possible directions:

- Multilingual schema labels or survey wording
- Low-literacy explanations of menstrual health data fields
- Offline-first or low-bandwidth collection ideas
- Interfaces that work for community health workers
- Accessibility reviews for forms, dashboards, or documentation

## Privacy And Ethical Data Use

Possible directions:

- Consent and withdrawal flows
- Privacy review checklists
- Aggregate-only analysis patterns
- Small-cell suppression approaches
- De-identification review workflows
- Clear explanations of what should never be public

## Partner And Community Infrastructure

Possible directions:

- Partner onboarding materials
- Data preparation guides
- Review workflows
- Community feedback loops
- Contributor profiles or acknowledgement systems
- Ways to make governance understandable to nontechnical partners

## Insight, Education, And Communication

Possible directions:

- Public-friendly explanations of synthetic or aggregate findings
- Responsible visualization patterns
- Educational resources about menstrual health data
- Plain-language summaries
- Policy or community briefing templates
- Ways to communicate uncertainty and limitations

## Privacy-Preserving Analytics

Possible directions:

- No-storage processing patterns
- Aggregate-only APIs
- Differential privacy demonstrations
- Federated or local-first analysis concepts
- Synthetic benchmark generation
- Risk review tools for public outputs

## Open Space

Participants may also work outside these areas. Strong contributions can be
technical, research-focused, governance-focused, design-focused, educational,
translation-focused, community-facing, or documentation-focused.

Good open-ended questions:

- What would make menstrual health research easier to do ethically?
- What would help communities understand how their data is used?
- What would help partners contribute better data without exposing people?
- What would make synthetic data more useful for testing?
- What would help future researchers compare menstrual health data responsibly?
- What would make the infrastructure easier for global contributors to adapt?
