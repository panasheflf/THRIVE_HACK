# Informed Consent Template - Menstrual Health Research

This is a template. Adapt it to local law, institutional requirements,
participant language, cultural context, and ethics board requirements before
using it with real participants.

Public prototypes should not collect real participant menstrual health data
unless an approved protocol, consent process, and data protection plan are in
place. For demos, use synthetic or fictional data.

## What is this study about?

## Is my participation voluntary?

Yes. Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may choose not to participate
or may withdraw at any time without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are
otherwise entitled.

## What are the risks?

This study involves questions about sensitive health experiences. Some questions
may feel personal, uncomfortable, or distressing. There is also a privacy risk if
health information is accidentally disclosed. The research team will use the
protections described below to reduce these risks.

## What are the benefits?

Your participation may not directly benefit you. Your contribution may help
improve menstrual health research, education, tools, resources, and care for
other people and communities.

## How will my data be protected?

- Your responses will be de-identified as early as possible after collection
- Your name will not be linked to your survey responses in published reports
- Data will be stored on secure, access-controlled systems
- Only authorized research team members will have access to identifiable data
- Data will be retained for [insert retention period] and then securely deleted
  or archived according to the approved protocol
- We will not sell your data
- We will not share your identifiable data with third parties for commercial
  purposes
- Any third-party tools used for processing, transcription, translation,
  storage, or analysis will be listed here: [insert third-party tools or "none"]

## Will my data be shared?

- De-identified data may be shared with approved researchers for future
  menstrual health studies
- Aggregate findings, such as regional summaries or broad trends, may be
  published in reports or academic papers
- Your individual responses will not be publicly released
- Any data sharing will comply with applicable data protection laws, ethics
  requirements, and data-use agreements

## Data Access Levels

Your data will be protected through the following tiered access model:

1. **Public Layer**: Open documentation, schema, code, and research methods; no
   personal data
2. **Synthetic Data Layer**: Fake datasets for development; no real participant
   data
3. **Aggregate Data Layer**: Carefully reviewed summaries that cannot reasonably
   identify individuals
4. **Controlled Research Layer**: De-identified data available only to approved
   researchers under strict agreements
5. **Private Raw Data Layer**: Original data remains restricted to authorized
   stewards; you may request access, correction, withdrawal, or deletion where
   applicable

## Your Rights

- You have the right to ask what data is held about you
- You have the right to request correction of inaccurate data
- You have the right to request deletion of your data where applicable
- You have the right to know how your data is being used
- You have the right to withdraw your consent at any time
- You have the right to ask questions or raise concerns
